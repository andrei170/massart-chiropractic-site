Our Story

Raised in chiropractic. Trained at Logan. Came home to Green Bay.

Dr. Timothea Massart grew up in a household where chiropractic was a normal part of staying healthy. Her mother worked for a local chiropractor, and the family participated in care the way some families participate in preventive medicine. That upbringing pointed her toward Logan College of Chiropractic near St. Louis, where she earned her Doctor of Chiropractic degree in 2001 after studying biology at Newcomb College of Tulane.

"I was a blessed child because my mom worked for a local chiropractor."

After a post-graduate mentorship year in Kentucky, she returned to Green Bay and opened Massart Chiropractic on West Mason Street around 2002. Twenty-four years later the practice is still hers, still independent, and still woman-owned. It has grown into a four-doctor team across two adjacent clinic addresses on the same block.

The reason the practice can offer next-day availability, a second-opinion option when you need it, and continuity across years of care is straightforward: it was built that way on purpose, by someone who knows what it means to grow up inside a practice that genuinely looked after its community.

How long has Massart Chiropractic been open?

Massart Chiropractic was founded in Green Bay around 2002 by Dr. Timothea Massart, DC, a Logan College of Chiropractic 2001 graduate. The practice has been on West Mason Street for over 24 years and is still independent and woman-owned.

How many locations does the practice have?

Two adjacent clinic addresses on the same block of West Mason Street in Green Bay: 1239 W Mason Street and 1315 W Mason Street. Both are part of the same independent Massart Chiropractic practice with the same four-doctor team.

How many doctors are on staff?

Four: Dr. Timothea Massart (founder and owner), Dr. Dane McCullough, Dr. Mitchell Bluml, and Dr. Jessica Gonzalez Monroe. Having four doctors on staff is what makes next-day availability and second-opinion access real options.

Is Massart Chiropractic part of a national chiropractic franchise?

No. Massart Chiropractic is independent, woman-owned, and owner-operated. It is not affiliated with any national chiropractic franchise or regional investor group. Care decisions are made on West Mason Street, by the practice.

What is Dr. Massart's background?

Dr. Timothea Massart, DC earned her Doctor of Chiropractic degree from Logan College of Chiropractic in 2001, with a biology background at Newcomb College of Tulane. She spent a year in Kentucky on a post-graduate mentorship before returning to Green Bay to open the practice around 2002.
